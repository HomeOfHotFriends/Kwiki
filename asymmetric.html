Asymmetry Studies: A Foundational Manuscript

ASYMMETRY STUDIES

Field, Relation, Interference, and the Fantasy Console as a General Theory of Asymmetric Formation
Authorial premise: This manuscript consolidates the prior treatise, formal specifications, and extensions into a single interdisciplinary document. It treats the proposed fantasy console not merely as a computing artifact but as a model system for a broader inquiry: how asymmetry is produced, stabilized, interpreted, and recursively reinserted across technical, biological, artistic, social, and material domains.

I. Why asymmetry deserves its own field

Across modern science, symmetry and symmetry breaking already serve as powerful explanatory ideas. In physics, symmetry considerations organize theory and symmetry breaking explains the emergence of differentiated states from initially more invariant conditions. In biology, symmetry breaking has become a central language for morphogenesis, cell polarity, tissue organization, and pattern formation, where a comparatively homogeneous condition gives rise to durable inhomogeneous structures. In art and aesthetics, asymmetry is not merely the absence of symmetry but a constructive resource for tension, vitality, and expert perception, with some evidence that art expertise shifts valuation away from simple symmetric preference.

Yet these literatures are usually partitioned by discipline. Physics treats asymmetry in relation to law and invariance; biology treats it as morphogenesis; cybernetics treats it as feedback, adaptation, and observer participation; art treats it as formal composition; computing often treats it as implementation detail, state divergence, race condition, or data irregularity. What remains underdeveloped is a field that treats asymmetry itself as a cross-domain object of study: how it is generated, how it persists, how it is read, how it is operationalized, and how technical systems can be designed to make asymmetry primary rather than residual.

Asymmetry studies, as proposed here, is therefore not a rejection of existing fields but a synthetic framework linking them. Its object is any process in which non-equivalence emerges, stabilizes, propagates, or acquires meaning across a substrate. That substrate may be physical matter, a cell membrane, a woven surface, a game board, a digital field, a conversational system, a social organization, or an artistic medium.

II. The fantasy console as model system

The source architecture proposes a coordinate-native relational execution system embodied as hardware: a fixed 2D execution field, canonically 19x19; SD cards treated as rule shards rather than storage; a structural sweep clock; a finite local state ontology; and an asynchronous controller that injects relations into an otherwise synchronous field. Its defining law is concise: one field, one function, one wave, one structural clock.

In standard systems language, this is a finite, deterministic, refresh-driven lattice machine with slot-parameterized rule composition and asynchronous external perturbation. In broader interdisciplinary language, it is a machine whose purpose is to let asymmetry be produced through traversal, slotting, interference, gesture, and accumulated deformation, rather than through random noise or imperative command sequences.

The 19x19 field is not just a display surface. It is the complete execution substrate: address space, identity space, and discrete value space collapse into coordinate. There is no whitespace and no inactive location; every coordinate is meaningful at every sweep. Stored value is minimized, while continuous intensity is derived from position and relation rather than stored as floating-point quantity.

This design is unusual because it makes structure fixed and variation emergent. Novelty comes not from an ever-expanding vocabulary of parts, but from changes in placement, sweep order, card interference, and controller injection. That is why the architecture matters for asymmetry studies: it operationalizes a general proposition that asymmetry can arise from repeated traversal of a simple bounded substrate under constrained local rules, and that such asymmetry can then be stabilized into reusable structure.
