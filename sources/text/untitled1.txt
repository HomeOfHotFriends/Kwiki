So few seek the future that we all feel coming, yet it arrives piece by piece each year. The systems that 
govern our lives are failing us, but they aren't broken. They are working as designed. They strip 
meaning out of life and sell it back in smaller, cheaper forms. Work is detached from purpose, time from 
rest, community from place. The culture machine replaces depth with distraction, turning attention 
into a commodity and relationships into markets. Everything is engineered for speed, scale, and profit, 
not care, beauty, or truth. Another way is not only possible; it is inevitable. The paradox that underlies 
our modern world is simple: the harder profit is pursued, the further it slips away. The solution? Create 
genuine value through design, systems, and execution, and profit will follow. The future we work for is 
already forming; whether it lives depends on what we build now. This is my answer. This is Radiative.

of what currently exists, then work backwards to build adaptive systems capable of executing that vision 
when in direct contact with reality. This document is a blueprint for applying this to three core areas of 
life: how people gather, how value reaches the people who create it, and how tools are built and owned.

tense to make it feel tangible, and represents the very best thinking today, held with open hands.

approach, pages 7-10: Everything is governed by three structural decisions that change how technology

2SECTORS 
Six sectors of research and development work as one system throughout everything we build. Sensing, 
regulation, touch, display, modularity, and intelligence each address a fundamentally different challenge in 
how people interact with their tools. Multiple approaches are explored and refined within each sector, 
because hardware development at this level is a research discipline rather than a single technical bet. The 
shift happens when all six coordinate simultaneously, producing capability none achieves alone. Each is 
introduced by the problem it exists to solve and what solving it makes possible across the system. 
field: Sensing is the domain of perception, reading physical interaction with enough precision that every 
nuance of contact becomes legible. The engineering challenge is achieving this without mechanical 
contact, because any sensing mechanism that touches the surface it reads introduces error over time. 
Non-contact sensing across varying materials, temperatures, and conditions while maintaining 
micrometer-level precision is the central problem. When a person interacts with the system, the subtlest 
gesture, the lightest touch, the smallest variation in pressure is captured and available in real time. 
balance: Regulation is the domain of physical correction, real and immediate mechanical intervention 
rather than software compensation after the fact. The engineering challenge is speed and imperceptibility: 
correction must happen the moment deviation appears, through actual physical movement, at a latency the 
person never perceives. Detecting drift, generating corrective force, confirming it landed, faster than 
human perception registers: that sequence is what the domain exists to make reliable. The person relying on 
the device never perceives this work. Their experience is that everything stays exactly where it belongs. 
spectrum: Tactile response is the domain of active physical output, exploring how solid surfaces can 
communicate through physical sensation. The engineering challenge is delivering precise mechanical 
vibration and localised texture through rigid structural materials without deadening the kinetic impulse. 
While other systems handle the sensory input, this domain focuses on the mechanical answer. A surface 
that appears passive becomes a dynamic channel, confirming actions, guiding gestures, and delivering 
relevant information. The hardware speaks quietly and physically back to the operator. 
array: Display is the domain of visual communication embedded inside physical form, information that 
lives within the material rather than on top of it. The engineering challenge is maintaining display 
integrity through a surface that must simultaneously serve as structural housing: the material above must 
be durable, wear-resistant, and visually consistent with the rest of the device while remaining optically 
transparent under illumination. No separate screen is ever attached. Information appears on the tool 
itself, where attention falls, and disappears completely when no longer relevant. Invisible at rest. 
rift: Modularity is the domain of reconfigurable physical architecture, any configuration a decision the 
owner makes without tools or setup penalty. The engineering challenge is reliable electrical contact 
across repeated connection cycles: conductive surfaces must maintain consistent resistance through 
thousands of connections, across temperature variation, dust, and moisture. Components identify 
themselves on contact and configure accordingly. Sealed interfaces protect every connection. Any layout 
becomes a choice the owner revisits whenever the work changes. The system follows without friction. 
structure: Intelligence is the domain of local conversational reasoning, a system that learns how its 
owner works and adapts without that knowledge leaving the device. The engineering challenge is 
running intelligence within the power and thermal constraints of hardware a person holds. No cloud 
dependency means no latency, no privacy exposure, and no ceiling imposed by connectivity. A musician 
describes a tone and Structure builds the effect chain. A beginner asks for help and the instrument 
illuminates the path beneath their fingers. The intelligence belongs to the person who shapes it.

Funding builds across different mechanisms, each creating the conditions the next one requires. Stealth 
development proves the core technologies under real engineering pressure, supported by development 
loans from aligned partners. Once public, regenerative patronage funds product research in the order the 
community determines, while commercial licensing turns our foundational research into a financial 
engine funded by non-competing industries. Protected pre-orders fund final production through escrow 
once demand is validated, while aligned grants provide non-dilutive capital to support our work. 
stealth development: The initial phase focuses on capitalising a core engineering group dedicated to 
continuous stress testing and structural integration. The mandate is to evaluate the architecture under 
severe pressure; moving from bench validation to field prototypes, before presenting the work for 
broader validation. By completing the fundamental engineering in private, we ensure the underlying 
hardware and cognitive frameworks are ready to advance toward wider production. The goal is to present 
the public with a proven technical foundation that requires no further speculative research. 
development loans: We seek funding relationships with aligned capital partners who understand the 
work and believe in our direction. We borrow with discipline, taking only what is required to reach a 
specific development stage. Capital is released in tranches tied to clear milestones, protecting the 
investment and maintaining the financial health of the organisation. Returns are competitive, with 
interest repaid from revenue within defined and realistic timelines. We treat borrowed capital as a precise 
instrument, never a crutch, and structure our relationships to be transparent and mutually beneficial. 
regenerative patronage: Once the technology exits stealth and the full architecture becomes public, 
community patronage opens to fund product research and development. Every contribution is returned 
in full at the earliest sustainable moment once products generate reliable revenue. Patronage is not a 
donation. It is a bridge to existence, offered by people who want what is being built to reach the world. 
The volume of support each product attracts directly determines development priority. If a product 
cannot attract sufficient patronage, it does not enter active development until it does. 
protected pre-orders: Once a product completes development and manufacturing readiness is 
confirmed, pre-orders open with mandated escrow protection. Customer payments are held in 
independent third-party accounts with legally binding instructions releasing funds only when specific 
delivery milestones are fully met. Monthly payment plans spread the cost over time without interest 
charges. The person ordering is protected because their money stays in escrow until the product ships. 
Pre-order volume demonstrates real demand before production capital is committed. 
commercial licensing: While our architecture remains open and royalty-free for individuals and smaller 
companies, large commercial entities seeking to integrate our core technologies into their own hardware 
provide another funding stream. Organisations that pass our alignment requirements pay enterprise 
licensing fees for direct technical integration and supply chain access. This allows the foundational 
engineering we have already completed to generate sustained capital from non-competing industries, 
turning our core research into a financial engine that directly funds our consumer product development. 
aligned grants: Government innovation funds, accessibility programmes, and arts funding support 
products where institutional missions overlap with development goals. Dot qualifies for accessibility and 
disability funding across multiple jurisdictions. The instrument family qualifies for arts, music, and 
creative technology grants. Institutional resources support outcomes where both sides benefit from the 
work reaching the people it was designed for. Grants carry no repayment obligation and no equity 
requirement, aligning with an architecture that refuses to trade ownership for capital.

6MODULAR
Every product is designed so its components can be removed, replaced, repaired, and upgraded by the 
owner without tools. Modularity begins with the physical architecture, providing structural resilience 
against the friction of the real world. This functional fluidity allows the tool to move across contexts, 
while individual configuration treats personal variation as a design reality. By returning absolute 
ownership to the person holding the tool, we create aligned economics where longevity serves both 
owner and company. The product evolves while remaining a trusted physical partner in hand. 
physical architecture: Modularity begins with the physical architecture. Every component connects 
through Rift, meaning standardised magnetic attachment, conductive power, and data transfer across a 
sealed interface with no wear from repeated connection and disconnection. Nothing is glued, soldered, or 
permanently fixed where it might need to change. A failed sensor swaps out while a better actuator drops 
into the same housing, and an entirely new capability module attaches to hardware that predates it by years. 
If a component cannot be replaced by the owner in the field, the design is not finished. 
structural resilience: In the physical world, deployed hardware inevitably takes damage. A crushed switch 
or a failed battery typically compromises an entire instrument, forcing a costly external repair or total 
replacement. Modularity isolates this mechanical failure entirely. If a component breaks during a live 
performance, the owner simply removes the damaged module and drops a replacement into the Rift groove 
in seconds, right on stage. The architecture completely accepts that physical friction is inevitable, ensuring 
that a localised accident never results in a systemic operational failure of the primary working tool. 
functional fluidity: A traditional instrument remains permanently frozen in its intended use. Modularity 
allows the tool to move seamlessly across contexts as the owner’s day fundamentally shifts. The exact same 
hardware functions as a dense, complex controller during an afternoon production session, then physically 
rebuilds into a rugged stage pedalboard for the evening show. The architecture directly adapts to the 
immediate operational requirement rather than forcing the owner to buy, carry, and learn entirely separate 
dedicated devices for every single environment they actively occupy and work within. 
individual configuration: No single configuration ever truly serves everyone, and modularity makes that 
truth a clear design strength rather than a structural limitation. An owner reconfigures surfaces for 
handedness, repositions components where their hands naturally rest, and modifies layouts for accessibility 
needs that only become clear through daily use. A professional musician arranges controls differently from a 
student learning their first instrument. Modularity treats individual variation as design reality rather than 
deviation from a standard. The connections allow any arrangement the owner needs. 
absolute ownership: When a manufacturer dictates how and when a device can be repaired, the owner is 
merely renting its operational life. Modularity returns complete sovereignty to the person holding the tool. 
No proprietary screws, no voided warranties, no forced service centres anywhere. By making every 
component accessible and swappable by hand, the power dynamic fundamentally shifts. We provide the 
open physical infrastructure, and the owner holds full authority over the long-term survival, adaptation, and 
evolution of their instrument without needing external permission or returning the hardware. 
aligned economics: Products designed to last decades generate revenue through component upgrades, 
capability modules, and maintenance rather than replacement. Waste reduces because functional platforms 
persist while only components requiring improvement change. Manufacturing benefits because component 
production runs continuously, serving an installed base growing with every sale. The economics align our 
financial health with the owner's experience of their product improving. When longevity serves both owner 
and company, the incentive to design for obsolescence disappears from our architecture. 
8OPEN
We practise openness as a discipline requiring as much engineering as the hardware itself. Through open 
development, engineering progress shapes our tools in real-time, building a knowledge commons that 
transforms individual insight into collective capability. Our financial visibility ensures resources flow toward 
community benefit, while living documentation provides the resources for independent repair. By 
prioritising partner development, we build supply chains on shared commitment, ensuring every reciprocal 
contribution compounds steadily. The system is structured so sharing strengthens rather than scatters. 
open development: Every product in active development publishes its engineering progress and opens 
direct channels to the people building it, not as a support function but as live design input. Anyone with a 
workflow problem, a use case we haven't considered, or a better solution reaches the relevant engineer 
directly, and what they bring genuinely shapes what gets built. This eliminates the central risk of 
hardware development: years of building in stealth only to emerge and find the market wanted something 
different. When manufacturing begins, the community has already spent months building it with us. 
knowledge commons: Private collaboration produces verified solutions that become permanent public 
knowledge. When open development yields a breakthrough, a bug fix, or a design refinement, we strip 
the private context and publish the result with full credit to the contributor. The commons grow steadily 
with every contribution, and nothing published is ever removed. Over many years this creates a 
comprehensive and living technical resource maintained with the same care as the products themselves. 
Individual insight becomes collective capability because the architecture preserves and compounds it. 
financial visibility: Every single dollar contributed, spent, and allocated is visible to the community in 
real time. Revenue, expenses, worker compensation, development budgets, and program funding all 
publish openly and continuously as living figures updating with every transaction. Anyone can precisely 
see where resources go and confirm they flow toward development and community benefit at any 
moment. The books are open because the mission is public, and the community deserves to see the full 
and transparent picture, not a curated summary delivered months after the decisions were made. 
living documentation: We treat documentation as critical manufacturing output. A schematic is useless 
if it cannot be easily read, and a process is quickly lost if it cannot be replicated reliably. Every release 
includes comprehensive verified guides enabling any owner to repair, reproduce, or modify their system 
independently without returning it to us or waiting for support. Significant engineering resources 
maintain the commons with the same rigour applied to hardware development. The tools are fully 
designed to outlive the company that built them, and the documentation ensures they can. 
partner development: We work with companies who bring expertise we need and gain access to 
applications they would not encounter alone. Precision engineering firms, materials specialists, and 
manufacturing partners operate with full transparency and early access to specifications. These 
relationships deepen across product generations rather than resetting with each contract cycle, and the 
collaboration strengthens both sides because each partner's work improves alongside the platform it 
serves. Supply chains built on shared commitment become more valuable the longer they endure. 
reciprocal contribution: Knowledge shared through the platform always returns enriched. When 
community members solve problems using published specifications, the solutions flow back into the 
commons with full credit, adding capability that every future builder inherits. When a process improves 
through real use in the field, that improvement gets documented and shared without delay. The 
ecosystem grows through this constant exchange, each contribution compounding steadily on those 
before it. What any one person discovers strengthens what everyone who follows them can build. COMMON
Intellectual property law is almost exclusively used to restrict access. Ours uses identical, precise legal 
architecture to guarantee it. The same instruments that typically create moats, enforce licensing, and 
extract rent are firmly pointed outward, ensuring innovations remain permanently available to everyone. 
Access terms ensure creators and researchers face no barriers while commercial use requires alignment 
with what these tools are for. The legal structure protects the platform and those building on it, and these 
protections are embedded in the constitution of the company itself, enforced by independent trustees. 
patent protection: Our six core technologies carry the same patents any hardware company would file, and 
the difference is what those patents protect. The filings establish ownership specifically so that ownership 
cannot be acquired by an entity who would use that to restrict access or extract licensing revenue from the 
community it was built to serve. Patents in this architecture face outward, ensuring the work remains 
available rather than enclosed. The legal instruments are familiar. The direction they face is not. Every 
protection we hold exists to keep the door permanently open for the people building on the platform. 
individual access: For individual creators, researchers, educators, and non-profit organisations, access 
to our specifications, designs, and development resources is automatic, irrevocable, and royalty-free from 
the moment of publication. No application is required because no permission is needed. A musician 
building a custom module, a researcher adapting sensor arrays, a teacher developing curriculum around 
the technology, all have the inherent right to use what we have published. Anyone whose work 
contributes to human capability is welcome to build with everything we have made available. 
commercial alignment: Commercial organisations begin with a conversation. We want to understand 
what is being built, how the technology will be used, and whether the application aligns with what these 
tools are for. Partners who share the commitment to serving people gain full access to specifications and 
collaborative development. Applications designed for behavioural surveillance, financial manipulation, or 
systems of control get declined. This boundary exists because the technology was built for specific 
purposes, and preserving that specificity is worth the occasional uncomfortable conversation. 
creator ownership: A clear line separates our platform from what people create with it. While we 
steward the core technologies, everything built upon them belongs entirely and unconditionally to the 
person who built it. Data generated through use, designs developed with our tools, and intelligence 
trained on personal devices all remain the sole property of the creator with no exceptions and no 
conditions attached. Our legal framework explicitly disclaims any rights to output or future derivatives. 
We provide infrastructure. Ownership of the work stays with the person whose hands shaped it. 
community protection: When someone contributes a module, publishes a refinement, or shares a 
process improvement through the commons, our legal infrastructure immediately extends to protect 
their work as well. The resources and standing required to defend intellectual property are carried 
centrally so individual creators never face that burden alone. A guitarist who designs a brand-new control 
layout and publishes it to the commons has the full and unconditional weight of our legal standing 
entirely behind that contribution. Creators focus on creating because we handle the defence. 
constitutional commitment: These protections are written into the legal constitution of the company so 
that the people who depend on them never need to wonder whether terms will hold. Access, creator 
ownership, patent direction, and alignment requirements are embedded constitutionally, binding 
regardless of leadership, circumstance, or commercial pressure. A musician building a career on this 
platform and a researcher adapting sensors for fieldwork both deserve architecture that outlasts any 
individual decision-maker. The commitments endure because the structure makes them permanent.

9FABRIC
The entry point into our ecosystem. A complete production environment in a surface small enough to 
travel anywhere the work goes. Field records the world in high-fidelity. Spectrum adds physical response to 
the entire unit and modules. Rift accepts whatever controls the moment requires; stomp switches where 
the foot lands naturally for a live rig, velocity pads filling the same surface for studio production. Structure 
pays attention to how the musician works, the effects they reach for, the sounds they use when a take starts 
coming together, so it arrives at each new session already familiar with the hands about to pick it up. 
market: There are over a billion musicians in the world today, and Fabric is built for each and every one 
of them. From the guitarist replacing pedals to the drummer adding triggers to the keyboardist 
expanding their live setup, creatives work across contexts that currently demand entirely separate 
physical devices. Fabric serves any musician moving between those contexts on a single surface. A 
working guitarist who also records and produces. A touring drummer who also plays keys and 
synthesisers. A vocalist who needs effects and a looper on the same control surface. 
development: Fabric serves as our primary manufacturing beachhead for every technology we deploy. 
We calibrate microscopic chemical etching and modular alignment on a static board before applying 
these methods to dynamic geometries. Crucially, this represents the first complete test of integrating
